## Linear Motion Simulation with VPython

A physics simulation built with VPython that lets students explore uniform linear motion and braking distance by directly modifying the code.
This simulation is designed for students to manipulate and experience linear motion, rather than just watch it. By editing specific variables in the code, students can:


- Observe how a car's braking distance changes depending on its initial speed and mass
- Set different conditions for two cars and compare their motion side by side


The goal is to help students connect the physics equation and real-life scenario to what they see on screen — change variables, run the code, and watch what happens.
Students learn more deeply when they can tinker with a model themselves. Instead of a fixed animation, the simulation invites students to form a hypothesis("What if the car is twice as heavy?"), test it by editing the code, and reason about the outcome.
